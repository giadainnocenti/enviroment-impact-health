The Data...


Air Quality Index (AQI)

The Air Quality chart displays state-level summary data from the EPA’s Air Quality Index (AQI) for reporting air quality. This index is an indicator of overall outdoor air quality and takes into account measured pollutants: Carbon Monoxide (CO), Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2), Ozone, Sulfur Dioxide (SO2), Particulate Matter 2.5 (PM 2.5), Particulate Matter 10 (PM 10), and Lead.

This chart depicts the overall AQI average along with the parameters that are most impactful on to COPD and asthma. They include, PMs 2.5 & 10, Ozone and Sulfur Dioxide (?).

For more information, visit the EPA Air Data page

Link to download source data: Air Quality System (AQS) API

Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS)

The CDC’s Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) collects state data about U.S. residents regarding their health-related risk behaviors and events, chronic health conditions, and use of preventive services.

For our purposes, the data paints the picture of the prevalence of diseases most affected by air quality: COPD and asthma. Using responses of two questions in the survey – we are able chart the prevalence of these two diseases at the state level over time.


For more information, visit the BRFSS page

Link to download source data: U.S. Chronic Disease Indicators: Asthma
